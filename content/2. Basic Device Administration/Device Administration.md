# Device Administration 

Device administration is the process of managing and controlling network devices like switches, routers, wireless access points, and gateways. It focuses on who can log in, what they are allowed to change, and how their actions are recorded. 
## Main Idea 

In simple terms, device administration uses AAA: authentication, authorization, and accounting. That means the system checks who the user is, decides what they can do, and keeps a record of their actions.
## Why It Matters 

This helps keep network devices secure and organized, especially in bigger networks where different people may need different levels of access. For example, one team might only view settings, while another team can fully manage the device.
